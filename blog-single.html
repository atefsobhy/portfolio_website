Biggest Journey of My Life

  • Duy Lan Le
  • Web Developer
  • 1

Traveling is always a good experience to most of us, we will have a change to see new things every days. However, we always want to return our home after days of traveling to countinues to work and meet our friends. In this blog, I want to tell you my journey about the biggest traveling time ever of my life.

I was born in Vietnam, a small country in Southeast Asia but have to stand against many big countries for thousands years. I have a normal childhood like other kids has. My family was a normal middle-class family. Then, one day, boom, I recieved a message from my father, he said:

I decided to go to the US to have a better life for you and your sister.

My father kept his plan for a long time because he didn't want to be distracted from school and running around for days. He want us to keep doing what I was doing, and he planned to go to the US for 2 years and then he will sponsorship my mom, my sister, and me later. After 2 years, The day has come. Three of us go to the US.

I have to say this, life in US is not beauty as in the Hollywood's movie. In Vietnam, I didn't have to do anything much to living, I had a job but I didn't have to take care family much, and I don't know anything else beside studying and playing video game.

When I live in US, I have to learn how to take care of our family for my father because I'm the only person can speak english in the house. Thus, I have to take care of the paper work, pay the bill, talk to my sister's teacher, work a full-time job, and study as a full-time student. My girl friend was in Vietnam at that time and we have a very hard time with each other lead to a break up. First year is a hard year to me to learn how to handle all of these things. Second year is better than first year when I get used to doing it.

After all, I think this is a biggest journey of my life, I learned many thing and I guess I grew up a lot, I think I became more mature than me of the previous 2 years. I had a lot of emotions, but I think life is already a journey for all of us. So that, I enjoy it instead of going against it.
